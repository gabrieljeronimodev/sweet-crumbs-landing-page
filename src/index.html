Our Story

Three generations of
flour and water.

It started in 1982 when my grandfather, Antonio, brought his 50-year-old sourdough starter from a small village in Italy. He believed that bread wasn't just food; it was the centerpiece of family life.

When we opened Sweet Crumbs in this exact neighborhood over a decade ago, we promised to honor his tradition. We refused to automate. We refused to use commercial yeast. We still mix our dough by hand, feeling the humidity and temperature of the air to know exactly when it's ready.

We aren't just baking bread. We are preserving an art form.

Do you remember what real bread tastes like?

Walk down any supermarket aisle, and you'll find shelves packed with "pastries" designed to last for weeks. They look perfect, but they lack soul. They lack the irresistible scent of browned butter and the delicate, shattering crunch of layers folded by hand.

At Sweet Crumbs, we don't believe in shortcuts. No dough conditioners, no artificial preservatives, and no mass production.

We believe your morning coffee deserves better. We believe you deserve a pastry that was crafted before dawn, specifically for you to enjoy today.
